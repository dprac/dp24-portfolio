About Me

My name is Daven Prachan. I am a Lao-American multidisciplinary artist.

I practice traditional illustration, painting, digital illustration, film editing, and photography.

My works involve evoking discussions around mental health, personal awareness, temporality, and identities.

Aside from these themes, I believe that the arts in general can help elevate struggles of the marginalized and issues often unseen in our everyday world.

Illustration

Illustration is my most developed art practice. I have experience in graphite, charcoal, and paint mediums.

Neo Gentifrication, 2021, Graphite on Paper

Arygle Alley and Uptown as a whole have special places in my heart as places that have shaped my childhood. I think of the stretches of Vietnamese, Thai, and other South East Asian buisnesses with fond nostalgia. Unfortunately, rapid urban developments, alongside closures during covid, have created the opportunities for gentrification to emerge in this area. I wanted to capture the rapid pace of this cultural erasure in all of its violence.

Ma Noi, 2023, Graphite on Gray Tone Paper

This work is a self portrait of sorts, based on my childhood nickname, "Ma Noi." This nickname roughly translates to "puppy", and it was given to me when I was a much more rambunctious child. I still feel like as an adult I am still seen as the puppy I was before, while still carrying the burdens of a grown up life. Just as the dog is blamed for eating homework, I too see myself baring unreasonable faults.

Digital Art

These works were created using digital software and coding in html, javascript, and css.

Important Question, 2024, coding via p5.js

During my last semester at UIC, I saw myself drowning in an overwhelming sea of mental health struggles. I was struggling to learn coding at the time, and aside from my already strong seasonal depression, I was also personally crushed by learning of my grandmother's failing health. I felt I there were days that I would be running on autopilot. I reflected upon these feelings and sought to manifest it through the imagery of a "software gore". A broken screen will reveal bleeding LEDs, and this work similarly reveals how broken I felt in this time.

Breaking News, 2024, html webpage

My semester progressed and I saw continued mental strain. My grandmother passed away and I had amassed a backlog of work in all of my classes. I was fortunate to recieve support, but I realized that I had to go long stretches out of my way to do so. I believe that there are mental health stigmas in my community, as well as persistent stereotypes that affect how likely people in my community are to recieve help. I felt that a piece of satire could best illustrate my feelings on how neglicted I felt my mental health issues were being addressed as an Asian American, so I wrote this article, which can be read here.

Photography

Wanderer, 2019, Digital Photography


This is a series of photographs taken before the onset of the global pandemic. The titlar wanderer reflects the state I felt at the time being in the middle of my 20-something life. Sometimes I feel that my life can seem aimless, or that I am constantly someone that is out of place or out of time. I still feel this way about my life, though I think being a wanderer does not have to be a bad thing from time to time.
